Living on Mars presents significant challenges due to its harsh environment. The Martian
atmosphere is mostly carbon dioxide, with very little oxygen, making it impossible for humans
to breathe without assistance. Temperatures are extremely cold, and there is no liquid water
readily available on the surface. Radiation from the Sun and space poses serious health risks due
to the lack of a protective magnetic field. To survive, humans would need advanced life-support
systems, habitats for shelter, and sustainable food and water sources. While colonizing Mars is a
long-term goal, current technology still requires significant advancements to make it feasible.
